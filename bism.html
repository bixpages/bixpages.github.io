Three Known Extensions of \(E = mc^2\)

Two Valid (Gravity, Electromagnetism) + One Conceptual (Higgs)
Jan Klein
Hannover, Germany · ORCiD: 0009-0002-2951-995X · janklein@engineer.com
Date: 19 April 2026 (final preprint version)

Abstract

Einstein’s energy–momentum relation \(E = \gamma m c^2\) describes a free particle in empty space. However, no real particle exists in isolation. Every particle interacts with fields. This paper reviews three known ways to extend \(E = \gamma m c^2\) to include such interactions, based on established and experimentally confirmed physics.

Extension 1 – Gravity (Einstein, 1915): \(E \approx \gamma (m c^2 + m \Phi)\), \(\Phi = -GM/r\).
Extension 2 – Electromagnetism (Maxwell–Einstein, 1905–1915): Exact: \(E = \sqrt{(\mathbf{p} - q\mathbf{A})^2 c^2 + m_0^2 c^4} + q\phi\)
Weak‑field approximation: \(E \approx \gamma m_0 c^2 + q\phi\).
Extension 3 – Higgs Mechanism (1964, confirmed 2012): \(m c^2 = y \cdot v\), \(v \approx 246\;\text{GeV}\).

The paper presents these three extensions in historical order, provides exact and approximate forms, domains of validity, and key experimental confirmations. No new physics is proposed. The goal is to collect and clarify what is already known: \(E = mc^2\) can be extended in exactly three well‑established ways – two direct (gravity, electromagnetism) and one conceptual (Higgs). This work is the conceptual launchpad to build a new extended energy equation.

1. Introduction

In 1905, Einstein derived \(E = \gamma m c^2\) assuming empty space. But no real body exists in vacuum: every particle moves through gravitational, electromagnetic, Higgs and quantum fields. The proton’s mass is 99% gluon field energy, not quark rest masses. How does the energy equation change when we include fields? Different fields enter differently; the strong interaction cannot be reduced to a simple potential. Nevertheless, three clear extensions exist, each rooted in textbook physics and experiment.

The three extensions are: Gravity (weak‑field limit adds \(m\Phi\)); Electromagnetism (minimal coupling yields \(E = \sqrt{(\mathbf{p}-q\mathbf{A})^2c^2+m_0^2c^4}+q\phi\)); and the Higgs mechanism (mass itself is \(y\cdot v/c^2\), a conceptual rather than additive extension). This paper is a reference for physicists and the necessary foundation for any future unification or novel extension of the mass–energy relation.

3. Extension 1: Gravity (Einstein, 1915)

Valid extension · weak‑field

Formula (weak, static field): \(E \approx \gamma m c^2 \left(1 + \frac{\Phi}{c^2}\right) = \gamma (m c^2 + m \Phi)\), \(\Phi = -GM/r\).

✔ Minor clarification: The exact expression from Schwarzschild is \(E = \gamma m c^2 \sqrt{1+2\Phi/c^2}\); for \(|\Phi|\ll c^2\) it reduces to the linear approximation above.

Domain: \(|\Phi|\ll c^2\) (Earth, Sun), static or slowly varying, test particle.
Confirmed by: Pound–Rebka (1959), GPS (daily), Hafele–Keating (1971), LIGO (2015–present).

4. Extension 2: Electromagnetism (Maxwell–Einstein, 1905–1915)

Valid extension · exact covariant

Exact formula: \(E = \sqrt{(\mathbf{p} - q\mathbf{A})^2 c^2 + m_0^2 c^4} + q\phi\).

Weak‑field approximation (\(\mathbf{A}=0\), \(q\phi \ll m_0 c^2\)): \(E \approx \gamma m_0 c^2 + q\phi\).

✔ Minor clarification: \(\gamma\) in the approximation uses the kinetic momentum \(\mathbf{p}_{\text{kin}}=\mathbf{p}-q\mathbf{A}\); for \(\mathbf{A}=0\) both coincide.

Domain: exact for all classical EM fields; approximation valid for weak electrostatic fields.
Confirmed by: Maxwell’s equations (all electronics), QED (most precise theory), particle accelerators (beam optics).

5. Extension 3: Higgs mechanism (1964 – confirmed 2012)

Conceptual extension · mass generation

Formula: \(m c^2 = y \cdot v\) with \(v \approx 246\;\text{GeV}\) (Higgs vacuum expectation value).
For W/Z bosons: \(m_W c^2 = \frac{1}{2}g v,\; m_Z c^2 = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{g^2+g'^2}\,v\).

✔ Minor clarification: Unlike gravity/EM, the Higgs field does not add energy to a pre‑existing mass; without the field (\(v=0\)) all elementary particles would be massless. The field generates the mass term itself.

Confirmed by: W/Z masses (1983, CERN), top quark mass (1995), Higgs boson discovery (2012, ATLAS/CMS), Yukawa coupling measurements (2012–present).

6. Summary of the three extensions

🔹 Gravity – Valid. Exact weak‑field: \(E = \gamma m c^2\sqrt{1+2\Phi/c^2}\) → approx \(E \approx \gamma(mc^2+m\Phi)\). Confirmed: GPS, Pound–Rebka, LIGO.

🔹 Electromagnetism – Valid. Exact: \(E = \sqrt{(\mathbf{p}-q\mathbf{A})^2c^2+m_0^2c^4}+q\phi\). Weak‑field approx: \(E \approx \gamma m_0 c^2+q\phi\). Confirmed: all electrodynamics, QED, accelerators.

🔹 Higgs – Conceptual. \(m c^2 = y\cdot v\) (mass generation). No additive potential. Confirmed: LHC 2012, W/Z masses, top quark.

Key observation: Gravity & EM add energy to pre‑existing \(m_0c^2\); Higgs generates \(m_0c^2\) itself. No single formula unifies all three. The strong interaction (QCD) does not admit a simple additive extension of this type.

🔬 Open questions – the missing pieces for a future extended energy equation
  • How does the strong interaction (QCD confinement, gluon field energy) enter the energy equation in a compact form?
  • Can gravity and electromagnetism be unified into a single covariant extension beyond the linear approximation?
  • What is the correct energy equation in quantum gravity (e.g., incorporating holographic or non‑perturbative effects)?
  • How do non‑linear field contributions modify the additive structure \(E = \gamma(m_0c^2 + \sum \kappa_i\Phi_i)\)?
  • Can the conceptual Higgs mechanism be reformulated as a structural additive term in a higher‑dimensional or effective field theory?

→ These gaps define the roadmap for building a truly new extension of \(E=mc^2\).

7. Conclusion

What have we learned? First, \(E = mc^2\) is not the final word when fields are present. Second, different fields require different mathematical structures. Third, the Higgs mechanism shows that even the concept of "rest mass" is field-derived. The three known extensions presented here are not contradictions of Einstein – they are consequences of taking his equation seriously in a world filled with fields.

Extension 1 (Gravity): weak gravitational potential adds \(m\Phi\).
Extension 2 (Electromagnetism): exact minimal coupling yields \(E = \sqrt{(\mathbf{p}-q\mathbf{A})^2c^2+m_0^2c^4}+q\phi\).
Extension 3 (Higgs): rest mass arises from \(m = y v / c^2\) – a conceptual, generative extension.

Together, they show that fields matter for the energy equation. This paper provides the conceptual basis upon which I am building a new extension of the energy equation. It is also intended to encourage other physicists to think further. Building on this foundation, the next step will be to propose a unified extension that combines the additive structure of gravity and electromagnetism with the generative insight of the Higgs mechanism, while addressing the open questions listed above.

The door is open.
